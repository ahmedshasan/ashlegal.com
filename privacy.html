Privacy Policy

How ASH Legal collects, uses, and protects information you share through this website.

ASH Legal (the Law Office of Ahmed S. Hasan, DBA ASH Legal) respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect through ashlegal.com, how we use it, and your rights regarding that information.

Information We Collect

When you use the contact form on this website, we collect:

  • Your name
  • Your phone number
  • Your email address
  • Any message or description you provide

We do not collect this information passively. You only share it when you fill out and submit the contact form.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information you submit solely to respond to your inquiry about possible legal representation. Specifically:

  • To contact you to schedule a consultation
  • To assess whether your matter is one we can help with
  • To provide a fee quote and discuss representation

We Do Not Sell or Share Your Information

ASH Legal does not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

Cookies and Third-Party Services

This website is hosted on Netlify and uses third-party services that may set cookies or collect anonymous usage data:

  • Google Fonts (font delivery)
  • Tailwind CSS CDN (styling library)
  • AOS animations CDN (scroll animations)
  • Netlify (hosting and form processing)

These services are governed by their own privacy policies.

Data Retention

Information you submit through the contact form is retained until your matter is closed or you ask us to delete it. Email ahmed@ashlegal.com to request deletion.

California Residents (CCPA)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act, California residents have the right to:

  • Request access to the personal information we have about them
  • Request deletion of that information
  • Opt out of any sale of that information — we do not sell personal information

To exercise these rights, email ahmed@ashlegal.com.

Children

This website is not directed at and does not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

Confidentiality and Attorney-Client Privilege

Submitting information through the contact form does not create an attorney-client relationship. Communications are not protected by attorney-client privilege until a representation agreement is signed. See the Disclaimer for full details.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "Effective" date at the top of this page reflects the most recent update.

Contact
