Practice Area

Misdemeanor Defense

Every misdemeanor deserves a real defense. A conviction is a criminal record — one that follows you for years.

Charges I Handle

Drug Possession

HS 11350, 11357, 11377. I fight for diversion programs (PC 1000, Prop 36) that result in full dismissal.

Trespassing

PC 602. The prosecution must prove you entered or remained willfully and without permission. Many arrests involve misunderstandings.

Resisting Arrest

PC 148. Pulling your arm away isn’t resisting. Asking questions isn’t delaying. Walking away from an unlawful detention isn’t obstructing.

Assault & Battery

PC 240, 242. Bar fights, road rage, misinterpreted interactions. Self-defense, mutual combat, and lack of intent are all viable defenses.

Disorderly Conduct

PC 647. One of the broadest and most abused charges in the Penal Code. The vagueness of the statute is both its power and its weakness.

Warrant Recalls

Outstanding bench warrant? I file a motion to recall it and get you back before the court without being arrested.

Paths to Keeping Your Record Clean

Pretrial Diversion

Many first-time misdemeanor charges qualify. Complete the conditions — community service, classes, counseling — and the case is dismissed.

Mental Health Diversion (PC 1001.36)

If a qualifying mental health condition contributed to the alleged offense, treatment-based diversion with full dismissal may be available.

Civil Compromise

For property-related offenses where the victim is compensated, the court can dismiss the case under PC 1377-1378.

Negotiated Reduction

A misdemeanor can sometimes be reduced to an infraction — eliminating jail time and significantly reducing the impact on your record.
